About Me

Hi! My name is Prashanti and I am currently a rising sophomore at Carnegie Mellon University in the School of Computer Science. I am a computer science major and either an additional major or minor in math. I love problem solving, algorithm design, discrete math, and typesetting proofs (its so satisfying!).

I am currently a STEP intern at Google with the Surveys team. I also am a teaching assistant at CMU. I was a TA for 15-122 (Principles of Imperative Computation) for Spring 2020 and will be a TA for 15-251 (Great Theoretical Ideas in CS) in the fall 2020 semester. My responsibilities for 15-122 included grading assignments, teaching a lab, and designing written assignments. I have also previously been involved with robotics through FRC and VEX.

In my free time I play lots of board/card games (Avalon, Settlers of Catan, Dominion, and more!), slowly make my way through the local library's science fiction and fantasy section, and waste time on Discord. I also play the flute and occasionally bring out my old Phantom of the Opera sheet music for piano when I'm bored.

I will read almost anything, but some of my favorites are Harry Potter, anything written by Brandon Sanderson, and I'll Give You the Sun. I'm a really big fan of the Cosmere and can often be found listening to Shardcast or browsing the Seventeenth Shard.

Projects and Experience

  1. FRC/VEX Robotics

    2. I was part of robotics for all four years of high school. I first joined my school's FRC team during my freshman year as part of the software subteam. As part of the team I worked on test driven development for our new flexible framework, autonomous pathing and robot control, self calibrating subsystems, and much more. My technical experience focused on effective testing and control theory.

    In addition, I lead outreach efforts such as our FTC and FLL mentoring program. The program supported two FTC teams with biweekly meetings and mentorship and started six FLL teams.

    In addition to my involvement with FRC, I was also part of the VEX program for six years. In tenth grade I started a VEX team at my high school to expand the robotics program and the number of students it could reach. The program included two VEX robots which competing anually in the VRC competition. They won the Judges award during my senior year.

    In addition to communicating with the school, securing space, recruiting members, and managing logistics, I was also heavily involved with the design, creation, and programming of the robots. I have significant experience with VEX design as well as leveraging my control theory knowledge from FRC to build a better product and help my teammates grow.

  2. Teaching Assistant at Carnegie Mellon University

    3. I am a teaching assistant at Carnegie Mellon Unversity. In Spring 2020 I was on the 15-122 course staff, where I helped grade assignments, taught a lab, and redesigned written assignments. The class teaches how to reason about code and correctness, basic data structures, and programming in C.

    In the fall I will be joining the 15-251 course staff. The class covers mathematical models of computation and provides a broad introduction to computer science theory including P vs. NP, approximation algorithms, randomized algorithms, Godel's Incompleteness Theorem, and a variety of other topics which vary by semester. This has been my favorite class at Carnegie Mellon so far and I'm excited to be a part of the team and for (hopefully) in person recitations!

    I hope to broaden and reinforce my knowledge of CS theory in this position by helping students and interacting with all the amazing, intelligent, and insanely talented other TAs. As I learn more about computer science theory I want to use this as a stepping stone to get involved with research in the area.

  3. STEP Intern at Google

    4. I am a summer 2020 STEP intern at Google with the Surveys team. My projects include this portfolio as well as a future capstone project in web development.
